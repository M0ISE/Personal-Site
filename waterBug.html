WaterBug

As part of my uni degree, I worked on an interface for an autonomous vehicle.

The Design Breif

“ our task as interaction designers is to map out the interactions that will be required to assist people in interacting with autonomous vehicles in future cities. ”

User Needs

We decided to research the user needs in the field of urban agriculture. And found that users wanted to have gardens and veggie patches, but they didn't feel like they had the time to take care of them in the long term.

  1. I want my plants to thrive
  2. I don’t have the time to maintain my garden
  3. I want something that is adaptive to my needs and my plants’ needs
  4. I don’t want to spend a fortune

In no particular order.

WaterBug

After going through 4 different design solutions, we settled on WaterBug. An autonomous land-based drone that waters, weeds, monitors and notifies users on the state of the garden. So that they can be more intentional with the time they have. We thought it was the strongest because it was the one the users understood the most and they enjoyed the idea and wanted to have one. And ultimately had the broadest possibilities for further development after.

Iteration on the design

Our task was then to design this interface. We decided on an iterative design process where we would mock-up, test it with users, Iterate and Repeat. In total, we went through 6 iterations from paper photos to hi-fidelity mockups.

The hardest part of the design was finding the right balance of information to display on the screen. But not overwhelming the user with it. After our different iterations, we decided to slip the UI into two parts.

A more straightforward, A press go to start with simple graphs and categorical data. And a granular Dataset driven UI that allows for maximum customisability and personalisation and information of what each particular plan is doing. This enables users to decide how much they want to get involved and see.

Testing

In testing, we found that users liked these two different levels. Saying that it didn’t feel like there were missing out on information they needed because they were overwhelmed by the amount of it at any level.

Reflection.

We designed an application that solves the users that we set out to solve.

But of course, it’s not perfect. Users suggested that it would be nice to create a custom dashboard so that they can Choose the exact right amount of data they wanted to see and have easy excess to. A place that they chose the data they cared about and organise it in a way that made sense to them. They also wanted more specific data and more explanation of what they needed to do in the app.
